Analysing the relationship between Socio-Economic factors and Mens and Womens National Football Team Performance

Introduction

My project looks at how Socio-Economic factors relate to Men's and Women's national football team performance, specifically attempting to understand if these factors can explain the difference between the strength of a Country's Men's and Women's Team. I think this is important as it may show how a Country's future development may influence their national teams, however, also look at Country's which are outliers and attempt to understand what they are doing right or wrong and how other Countries can learn from this. I will be using the Percentage Difference between Country's Men's and Women's National Teams FIFA ranking points as this makes comparisons between country's easier as ranking points are not in nominal terms.

Conclusion

In conclusion it is clear that there is a relationship between socio-economic factors and Men’s and Women’s national football team performance. More socially and economically developed countries are more likely to have better women’s teams and better women’s teams relative to their men’s teams. The strongest relationship observed is between Gender Equality and HDI vs Women’s national team performance clearly an indicator suggesting that. I identified Mauritius and Brazil as anomalies, with Brazil having a very strong women’s team despite relatively average gender equality and economic development. This can be explained by its large population and strong men’s team which suggests high popularity and infrastructure for football. Mauritius was a harder anomaly to explain but perhaps is overexaggerated in my charts as there are numerous countries which do not have a ranked women’s team which could not be included. The choropleth chart also showed interesting regional differences with Scandinavia, South-East Asia representing areas where Women’s football was proportionally better than Men’s. In contrast the Middle East and North Africa regions where are many countries have no ranked Women's team showing areas where Men’s football dominates Women’s.

Accessing Data

I used data from FIFA to measure Men’s and Women’s national football team performance, this was because their ranking points are the most widely used and comparable measurement of national football team performance. The FIFA website blocked scraping however, I was able to copy their table into a google sheets file which I exported as a csv. I used World Bank data for population and GDP which I accessed using the World Bank API python package downloading using the pip install method. This is useful as it means when World Bank data updates I won’t have to change any code as the API will automatically update. I downloaded UN data for their Human Development Index and Gender Inequality from the UN website which was relatively simple, I chose these indexes as they are two of the most commonly used development and gender equality indicator and contained data for almost all the countries which had ranked national football teams.

Data Analysis

In performing my data analysis data cleaning was my biggest problem especially with regards to country names. I mainly overcame this using the ‘outer’ method in the pandas merge function, this allowed any matching errors to become clear as once merged there would be rows with missing data which were usually matching errors. I was then able to manually remove these errors in Excel by exporting the csv in Pandas. As well as this merging my national football team dataset with an alpha codes dataset when producing my choropleth map was useful as it meant merging World Bank datasets was simple as they also contained alpha codes removing the matching issue. In making my choropleth map I preferred the plotly package over vegalite as I found it easier in dealing with missing data which is shown in my choropleth map in grey but in vegalite as white the same as the ocean. Furthermore, I also preferred the statsmodels package over scikit-learn when doing regressions as it provided p-values making it easy to establish statistical significance helping me to understand relationships better.
